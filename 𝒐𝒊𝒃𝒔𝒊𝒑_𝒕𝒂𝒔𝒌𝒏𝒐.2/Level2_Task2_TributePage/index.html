SACHIN TENDULKAR

Sachin Tendulkar is an Indian former international cricketer and a former captain of the Indian national team. He is widely regarded as one of the greatest batsmen in the history of cricket.

About Him

Known as the "God of Cricket" and "Master Blaster," Sachin Tendulkar is renowned for his extraordinary achievements and contributions to the sport. He holds numerous records, including being the highest run scorer of all time in International cricket.

Tendulkar made his Test debut against Pakistan in 1989 and went on to play for over two decades. He was the first player to score a double century in a One Day International and holds the record for the most centuries in both Tests and ODIs.

In 2010, Time magazine included Tendulkar in its annual Time 100 list as one of the "Most Influential People in the World". He retired from cricket in 2013, leaving behind a legacy that continues to inspire future generations.
